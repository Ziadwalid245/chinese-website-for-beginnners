Useful Websites

Chinese Grammar Wiki

Chinese Grammar Wiki is a really great website to understand confusing grammar points, they cover common beginner questions Like how to use 有 and 也, difference between the negation using 沒 and 不, and even intermediate and advanced topics (Although the advanced section is still being edited) like how to use 地 and 得, how to use 把 and 被 and how to use 似乎

The explanations are brief, straight to the point and with a large number of example sentences this is an indispensable resource for any Chinese learner.

Forvo

Forvo is a site where you can listen to native speakers pronounce certain words . I find that pretty useful as Chinese has a lot of two syllable words and one tone could change depending on the tone after it.

  • For example: When a 3rd tone is followed by another 3rd tone in a group, the first 3rd tone changes to a 2nd tone like : 你好will be written as (nǐ hǎo) ,but will be pronounced as （ní hǎo）.
  • When followed by a 4th tone, 不 (bù) changes to 2nd tone (bú). Like: 不对(bùduì) will be pronounced with two 4th tones but pronounced as “búduì.”
  • When followed by a 4th tone, 一 (yī) changes to 2nd tone (yí) Like : 一个 (yīgè) will be pronounced as “ yígè ”.
  • when followed by any other tone, (yī) changes to 4th tone . like: 一起 (yīqǐ), will be pronounced as “yìqǐ.”
So it’s pretty helpful to hear native speakers pronounce the words.

Line Dictionary

Line is a pretty decent dictionary to use if you’re on a pc or a laptop most of the time, the dictionary not only shows you how to write each word when you look it up（some mobile apps like hangping make that a paid feature）, it also has a way for you to write characters you don’t know by click on the brush icon next to the search icon, but isn’t the smoothest thing to use on a computer, still it’s one the best online dictionaries I’ve managed to find

I would also recommend downloading hangping or pleco to use on your phone.

Baidu Translate is a a great site for translating sentences from English , Spanish , German and a variety of languages to and from Chinese and is surprisingly accurate (I prefer it over google translate) and is a great way to check if your sentences are correct or not , it works really great for small sentences and paragraphs , but if you want to translate an entire essay or short story in Chinese you start to have some problems.

personally when I translated large texts ( articles on historical figures , historical events , poetry , or even just some dialogue ) some words wouldn’t be translated , I would end up with weird sentences like :"After graduation from college, my husband met his current husband, and then fell in love and got married, and became a housewife."

All in all a great resource to correct simple sentence and understand the basic theme and concepts of large text, but wouldn’t rely on it too much.

You can also download the mobile app ( from a pretty sketchy Chinese site) to take pictures of an entire document or text and it will give you a translation for it .
