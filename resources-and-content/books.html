Books

books are a pretty essential tool for learning any skill. the way i see it there are no "good" or "bad" books when youre learning a language because i think all books will give you a more organised way to practice and approach learning grammar and vocabulary wether it is through lessons and example dialouges or just through the old way of writing the grammar rule and give you example sentences , all books are a valuable way to to practice and reinforce concepts (which is pretty important).

a peice of advice that i can give in regards to using the books below or any book you wanna buy for that matter is that after going through a lesson or two and learning some vocabulary , you may feel like you have a solid grasp on the material and the language , but you dont , learning a language isnt just about memorising some phrases or getting through a lesson its about being able to use what youve learned in an actual conversation , so id recommend after youre done with a lesson practice the excersices given the excersise will show where your true weaknesses lie and what you really understand and what to improve on .

there no 'bad' language books and that is true , but if a language book doenst let you practice what you learned , its pretty lacking in quality

this is alos a great book , it explains beignner level all the way to advanced grammar points from how to use 是 and 要 all the way to how construct passive sentence with 被 , and the diffrent uses of 就 . can find it here along with its workbook

this book has a pretty simple way of explaining complicated grammar points and all the sentcnes and example sentence have pinyin and exnglish translations so you wont suffer looking words up .
