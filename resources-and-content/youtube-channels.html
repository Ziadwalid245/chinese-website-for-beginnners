Grace Mandarin Chinese

Grace Mandarin chinese is a great channle for beginners , she has a simple way of explaining complicated topics from grammar points like æŠŠ to tounge positions when pronouncing syllables , she also has some vlogs that she posted that have chinese subtitles (no pinyin) and english translation , and theyre a great way to practice your listening skills .

i would recommend watching her video for Chinese sentence structure and must-have learning tools as a start , and if youre feeling brave check out her chinese pronounciation playlist especially helpful if youre recording your voice and comparing it to natice speakers whch is a good habit to get into early ( peice of advice dont pick too many videos to copy , just one and stick to it ).

she also has some good TV show recommendations.

Mandarin Corner

Mandarin Corner is a great channel to practice your listening skills , it has a large variety of videos ranging from the host asking chinese people on the street history trivia or what they think about dating to doing one-on-one interviews that about aspects of chinese culture like chinese declining birth rate or just an interview about what its like being a taxi driver in the worlds most populated country .

the chanel alos has an HSK course that is pretty extensive and gose through most if not all the vocabulary and grammar from HSK 1 - HSK 6 .

Down To Earth Chinese

Down To Earth Chinese is a channnel made by jared and su-su , where they break down chinese slang and terms in a fun and entertaining way , jared also has his own channel where he does vlogs and tries out random chinese snacks , its also in chinese with english subtitles and its pretty fun and serves as way to just have fun as opssoed to hammering chinese grammar and vocabulary in your braib all day

Everyday Chinese

Everday Chinese is a channel that is mostly grammar focused , they have a great way of explaining confusing and/or complicated grammar points and structures , a peice of advice though , and forget what you actually wanted to know in the first place( that never happened to me , a friend told me )

avoid jumping from one grammar video to the next , only watch a video about the grammar point you dont understand and move on , otherwise youll overwhelm yourself and wont learn anything.

Little Fox Chinese - Stories & Songs for Learners

Little Fox Chinese has mostly short stories and kids rhymes that are rarely longer than 10 minutes , the videos are spoken slowly , are easy to follow along and use simple chaacters which is great if you are a beginner , as most chinese content i found were either too fast to follow or hard to understand as they used alot of complicated charatcers , lucky for you you wont find any of that here ,

this channel is a great place to start moving away from using pinyin and english translations to understand videos and instead use your own knowledge to understand whats going on , and the easy content and slow pace will help you do just that.
